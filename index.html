About Allama G.A. Parwez

Allama Ghullam Ahmed Parwez (1903-1985) was a prominent Islamic scholar and author known for his critical analysis and interpretation of the Quran. He contributed significantly to the understanding of Islam's fundamental principles and their relevance in the modern world.

This educational website aims to preserve and promote his valuable works, including Quran lectures, books, and special lectures, to enrich the knowledge of Islamic teachings among the masses.

Parwez's contributions revolve around his critical analysis of the Quran and his efforts to promote rational and progressive interpretations of Islamic teachings.

Contributions

1. Quranic Exegesis

Allama Ghulam Ahmed Parwez devoted much of his life to studying and interpreting the Quran. He believed in approaching the Quran from a rational and logical perspective, emphasizing its coherent message and removing any inconsistencies that may have arisen through traditional interpretations. He advocated the Quran as a complete and comprehensive book, addressing all aspects of life.

2. Rejecting Sectarianism

Parwez strongly rejected sectarian divisions within Islam and promoted the idea of a united Muslim community based on the Quran's principles. He emphasized the Quran's call for unity and warned against the divisive impact of sectarianism.

3. Concept of "Nizam-e-Rabubiyyat"

Parwez introduced the concept of "Nizam-e-Rabubiyyat," which refers to the Divine System of governance described in the Quran. According to him, this system advocates social justice, equality, and welfare for all, and it serves as an alternative to traditional forms of governance.

4. Emphasis on Reason and Science

Parwez stressed the importance of reason and rationality in understanding the Quran. He advocated that science and reason should go hand-in-hand with religious knowledge to form a comprehensive worldview.

5. Advocate of Ijtehad

Parwez was a strong advocate of Ijtehad, which refers to independent reasoning and interpretation within Islamic jurisprudence. He believed in the continuous relevance of Islamic principles to contemporary issues and encouraged scholars to engage in ijtihad to address the challenges of the modern world.

6. Publications

Allama Ghulam Ahmed Parwez authored numerous books and pamphlets in both English and Urdu, which discussed various aspects of Islamic teachings and his unique interpretations of the Quran. Some of his notable works include "Lughat-ul-Quran," "Islam: A Challenge to Religion," and "Mafhoom ul Quran."

7. Founder of Tolu-e-Islam Movement

Parwez founded the Tolu-e-Islam movement in Pakistan to propagate his ideas and teachings. The movement aimed to promote an enlightened and rational understanding of Islam, focusing on the Quran as the primary source of guidance.

About

For over three decades, this non-profit website has been dedicated to gathering and organizing the works of Allama G.A. Parwez, aiming to promote the message of the Quran and encourage critical thinking.
